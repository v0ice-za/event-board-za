# Product Brief: Event Board ZA

## Executive Summary

Johannesburgers have nowhere good to look for events. The options that exist — Google searches, Facebook Events, whatsoninjoburg.com, Quicket — are fragmented, web-first, or too generic to feel local. Event Board ZA is a mobile app that aggregates all events happening in Johannesburg into a single, clean, card-based feed. Each card shows the event name, date, and venue at a glance; tap for the full picture.

It's the one place JHB residents check to answer "what's on this weekend?" — for every kind of event, every kind of night out. The app launches JHB-first, free to use and ad-supported, with a clear path to expand to other South African cities.

## The Problem

Finding events in Johannesburg is more effort than it should be. There's no single habit-forming destination. The closest thing — whatsoninjoburg.com — is a web-first editorial site, not a mobile-native feed. Facebook Events is noisy and algorithmically filtered. Quicket and Computicket are ticketing platforms, not discovery tools. Global aggregators like AllEvents and 10times cover JHB but feel generic, not local.

The result: people miss events they would have loved, default to the same handful of venues they already know, or give up on planning entirely because discovery is too fragmented and too much effort.

The pain cuts across all event types — concerts, markets, food pop-ups, art shows, sports, comedy nights, club nights — and every kind of social occasion: couples, friend groups, solo outings.

## The Solution

A mobile app with a single feed of events happening in Johannesburg. Clean card-based UI — each card shows the event name, date, and venue. Tap for full details: description, time, location, price range, and a link to tickets where applicable.

No editorial noise. No social graph filtering what you see. No generic global platform that treats JHB as one city among thousands. Just events, clearly presented, built for the phone in your pocket.

## What Makes This Different

- **Mobile-first**: Every comparable option is web-first. This is designed for the device people actually use to plan their social lives.
- **JHB-centric**: Not a global platform with a JHB filter. Local focus means local relevance — community markets, free gigs in Maboneng, city-specific events that global aggregators miss or list late.
- **All event types, one place**: Concerts, markets, food events, art, sport, comedy, family — no vertical niche. Users don't need to know what kind of night they want before they browse.
- **Simple by design**: The MVP resists feature creep. The job is discovery, not ticketing, not social networking, not content publishing.

[ASSUMPTION: Competitive advantage holds as long as no well-funded direct competitor enters the JHB mobile-first events discovery space before meaningful traction is established.]

## Who This Serves

**Primary — the JHB social planner**: Smartphone-native Johannesburg resident, late 20s to mid-40s. Plans weekends with a partner, friend group, or solo. Wants to know what's on without doing research. Doesn't want to miss the good stuff. Frustrated that discovery currently requires checking multiple apps and websites.

The product was born from the founder's own friction — that's the canonical user persona.

**Secondary (later)**: Event organisers who want their events in front of an engaged local audience; advertisers who want to reach an active, out-and-about JHB demographic.

## Success Criteria

- Users open the app at least weekly (not just when they happen to remember it)
- Event listings cover all major categories with no glaring gaps that push users back to Google
- Measurable attendance attribution — at least some users attend events they found here
- [ASSUMPTION: 1,000 monthly active users within 6 months of public launch as an early traction signal]

## Scope

**In for MVP:**
- Johannesburg events only
- Card-based feed (event name, date, venue visible without tapping)
- Event detail view (full description, time, price, ticket link)
- Basic category filtering (music, markets, food, art, sport, comedy, family, etc.)
- Ad-supported monetisation
- iOS and Android

**Explicitly out for MVP:**
- Other cities (roadmap item)
- User accounts and saved events
- Social features
- Event organiser self-serve submissions
- Push notifications and reminders
- In-app map view
- In-app ticket purchasing

## Open Questions

1. **Data sourcing** — How does event data get into the app? Candidates include Quicket/Eventbrite API integration, manual curation by the team, organiser self-submission, or automated scraping. This is the single biggest technical and operational unknown and must be resolved before build begins.
2. **Monetisation depth** — Ads are the starting point. Promoted listings (organisers pay for top placement) and affiliate revenue from ticket sales referrals are worth evaluating once a user base exists.
3. **Content bar** — Who decides what gets listed and what doesn't? Is everything in, or is there a quality threshold?

## Vision

Event Board ZA becomes the default way Johannesburgers discover what's on — the app you open Thursday night to plan your weekend. Once JHB is working, the model expands to Cape Town, Durban, and beyond. At scale it becomes South Africa's go-to local events platform: the place event organisers want to be listed, and the place advertisers reach an active local audience.
