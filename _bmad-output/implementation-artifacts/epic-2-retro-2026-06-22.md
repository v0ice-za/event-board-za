## What Went Well

1. **Reuse discipline held.** Every story opened with a "do NOT reinvent" section and it paid off ‚Äî `CategoryPlaceholder ‚Üí EventCard ‚Üí FeedScreen` composed without a single duplicated gradient/emoji map or a second `QueryClient`. The `eventKeys` factory was used everywhere; no ad-hoc query keys leaked in.
2. **Learnings compounded between stories.** The `Animated`/lint gotchas discovered the hard way in 2.2 (lazy `useState` not `useRef().current`; never `setState` in effects) were pre-empted in 2.3 ‚Üí *"zero lint/tsc errors on first run."* Velocity gained directly from prior-story memory.
3. **Adversarial review caught real defects, not nitpicks.** Empty-string `imageUrl` ‚Üí broken `<Image>`; `imageError` never resetting on recycled cards; `doc.id` clobbered by an external-feed body `id` field; `formatDate` rendering "undefined undefined undefined" on bad input. All killed pre-merge.
4. **Testing discipline was consistent and monotonic.** Co-located tests, per-file mocks, `jest-expo`, no brittle animation-frame assertions ‚Äî the pattern set in 2.1 held across all six stories, with the suite growing every story and never regressing.

## Challenges & Growth Areas

1. **üî¥ On-device verification debt is now epic-wide.** *Every* story deferred its visual/interaction QA (8 placeholder variants, gradient/shimmer, chip return-pulse, offline banner, foreground refresh) because there is no simulator in the dev environment. Epic 2 is marked `done` but **has never run on a real device.** This is the single biggest carry-forward risk and compounds into Epic 3's inherently visual feed‚Üídetail‚Üíback + webview flows.
2. **üüÝ Architecture doc has drifted from what shipped.** Three stories found `architecture.md` superseded by the epics/UX spec: `FilterChips ‚Üí CategoryChip/ChipsRow` (2.3), `ErrorState ‚Üí EmptyState` variants (2.4), and `firestore() ‚Üí getFirestore()` (2.5). Practice has been "epics + UX spec are canonical," but the architecture doc now misrepresents the codebase.
3. **üüÝ Recurring "unguarded external-feed data" theme.** Surfaced as a review finding in 2.1, 2.2, 2.5, and 2.6 ‚Äî each correctly deferred to "Epic 4 `normaliseEvent`." Right call, but the debt is concentrated at the Firestore read/trust boundary and is now referenced from four stories' deferred lists. Epic 4 must actually close it.
4. **üü° Review occasionally trended toward gold-plating.** 2.4 produced 10 patches on a presentational component (try-catch + fallbacks throughout). Worth keeping review findings proportionate to a component's actual risk.
5. **üü° Process: all Epic 2 work is uncommitted on `main`.** Twelve files, no checkpoint history.
